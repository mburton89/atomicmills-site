FAQ

Is Bearplane really inspired by a true story?

Believe it or not, yes! Bearplane takes loose inspiration from Wojtek, the real-life bear who served alongside Polish soldiers in World War II. Our version adds a few more jet packs and shenanigans :D

What was the development of Bearplane like?

Bearplane took flight in 2020 during lockdown. With travel and work suddenly off the table, I found myself deep in the weird corners of history, where I discovered the incredible story of Wojtek. It was bizarre, funny, and strangely heroic… I HAD to make a game about it.

I was inspired by the tonal contrast in Fallout—brutal combat wrapped in cheerful 1940s vibes. I wanted Bearplane to feel like that too. I also borrowed from Skyrim’s shout mechanic and the physical comedy of the cartoon Tom and Jerry, where the audio always perfectly matches the slapstick mayhem.

For two years, I focused entirely on getting the movement and core mechanic to feel just right.

By 2023, I teamed up with composer Sean Smith, who created the game’s big-band score and recorded it with a real jazz band to capture the spirit of the era. I also brought on incredible artists Leo Siebert, Jordan Wadell, and Vinnie Peters, who helped bring Bearplane to life visually. I couldn’t be happier with how well the team worked together—everyone brought their unique strengths and creative energy, and the final result surpassed all expectations. It was truly a dream collaboration.

Since then, over 7,000 hours (and cups of coffee) have gone into turning this very reasonable idea of a bear with a jetpack into a fully playable game.

Who or what on earth is Atomic Mills?

Why, it’s only the most cutting-edge food and beverage company this side of the war effort! Atomic Mills is the (totally fictional) manufacturer behind bold wartime favorites like Nukey Nectar, Honey Thunder Cola, and Flappy Flakes Breakfast Cereal. Founded during the heyday of World War II, Atomic Mills was dedicated to using the limitless potential of nuclear energy and the hard work of radioactive bees to give our brave soldiers a nutritional advantage on the battlefield.

Fueled by patriotism and a suspicious orange glow, Atomic Mills delivered explosive flavor and questionable science in every bite.

Is Bearplane single-player or multiplayer?

Both! You can fly solo or connect a controller for local co-op. Player 2 controls Rocket Rat and his trusty rifle. Team up and double the firepower!

What devices and controls are supported?

On mobile, play with touch controls or a gamepad. On PC, use mouse, keyboard, or a controller. Bearplane also runs great on Steam Deck.

Does Bearplane run on Steam Deck?

Bearplane runs exceedingly well on Steam Deck. In fact, a lot of the focus for the PC version has gone into optimizing it specifically for Steam Deck, ensuring buttery-smooth performance at 90 FPS.

The gamepad layout should be set by default. If it’s not, we recommend switching the controller layout type to "Gamepad" in your Steam Deck settings for the best experience.

What’s the difference between the Steam and mobile versions of Bearplane?

The core gameplay is the same, but there are a few key differences:

  • Steam version: A one-time purchase of $4.99 unlocks the full experience right away. No in-app purchases.
  • Mobile version: Free to start. You can unlock the full game for $4.99, which includes:
    • A wardrobe of special hats with powerful, unique abilities
    • Double rewards from missions (like Nukey Nectar and Flappy Flakes)
    • The ability to skip missions
    • Access to leaderboards and achievements
    • 10% more cartoon blood (you’re welcome)
    • Bonus: Plug in a controller and play as Rocket Rat, while Bearplane follows and auto-attacks!

How do I unlock the full game on mobile?

Just tap the “Buy Full Game” button on the main menu to get all the goodies and unleash the full Bearplane experience.
